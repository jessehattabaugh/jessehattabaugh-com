What is Integer Scaling?

Integer scaling is a method for scaling up emulated games to a higher resolution without introducing blurring. It works by duplicating each pixel in the image a number of times to fill the space of the higher resolution image. This method is used in emulators to scale up the resolution of old video games to modern displays.

But different emulators have different screen resolutions, and the original image doesn't always scale up to the same size. This can cause letterboxing or pillarboxing, which is when black bars appear on the sides or top and bottom of the screen. This wastes your screen space and can be distracting.
